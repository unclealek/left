# Left

A mobile social discovery app built around presence, places, and spontaneous real-world connection.

---

## Project Overview

- **Project:** Mobile application
- **Scope:** UI/UX, product design, and brand direction
- **Platform:** iOS and Android
- **Focus:** Social discovery, real-world interaction, venue-based presence, safety-forward connection

Left is a social discovery app designed to help people meet naturally in shared physical spaces.

Instead of endless feeds, follower counts, and identity-heavy online personas, Left helps people discover who is nearby, what they are open to, and whether a real-world conversation feels worth starting.

The product is built around temporary visibility, soft anonymity, and venue-based context. Users do not broadcast to the internet at large. They become discoverable only when they intentionally activate a session in a shared place.

---

## The Problem

Most social platforms optimize for attention rather than connection.

People spend hours consuming content, but that rarely translates into meaningful interaction with new people in the real world. Existing social apps often rely on public profiles, swipe mechanics, or high-friction outreach that makes connection feel performative, transactional, or awkward.

This leads to:

- Digital loneliness despite constant connectivity
- Pressure to curate an online identity
- Missed opportunities to connect with people already nearby
- Difficulty knowing who is actually open to conversation
- Social tools that keep people on their phones instead of helping them look up

People are often surrounded by potential connection, but lack a low-pressure way to discover it.

---

## The Solution

Left turns shared places into lightweight opportunities for real-world connection.

When users enter a venue, they can choose whether to become visible for a limited time. Before activating, they set a current intention, choose up to two vibes, and optionally add a short identifying hint.

Discovery happens through:

- Shared places
- Shared interests
- Shared intentions
- Real-world presence
- Contextual compatibility

Instead of matching people through static profiles or endless browsing, Left helps users evaluate who is nearby in the moment and decide whether to approach.

The goal is not to prolong digital interaction. The goal is to reduce friction and support a real conversation offline.

---

## Example Use Case

A person sitting in a cafe and open to meeting others can activate a short visibility session with an intention like `Networking` or `Open to chat`, select vibes such as `AI/startups` or `Design`, and discover other nearby people who are also visible in that same venue.

From there, they can view a lightweight profile, use a saved approach prompt, and decide whether to start a conversation in person.

## Core Product Principles

### Presence Over Profiles

Who a person is open to being right now matters more than a fully constructed online identity.

### Intent Over Opaque Algorithms

Users explicitly choose why they are visible instead of relying on black-box recommendation systems.

### Discovery Over Swiping

People discover one another through place, timing, vibes, and context rather than appearance-first interactions.

### Real-World Connection Over Screen Time

The app should help people spend less time inside the product and more time engaging with the world around them.

### Safety As A Product System

Visibility is temporary and intentional. Identity stays light. Users can hide, block, report, pause, or end their session at any time.

## The Big Idea

People do not need another feed.
